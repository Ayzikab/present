Front-end Vs. Back-end development

Web Development is primarily categorized into Front-end development and Back-end development. So, if you plan to start a career in web development, you should know what both terms mean.
Front-end developers work on what the client can see while back-end developers fabricate the infrastructure that upholds it. The term "front-end" refers to the UI, while "back-end" signifies the server, application, and database working in the background to send the data to the client.

The back-end, also called the server-side, comprises the server that delivers information on request, the application that channels it, and the database that arranges the data.
Thereâ€™s another term called Full-stack development, which is nothing but the combination of both front-end development and back-end development. A full-stack developer is someone who can design and create both the front-end and back-end of an application.

So you can see, you have three options here from career perspective in web development. You can start your career as a front-end developer, or a back-end developer or you can learn both and be a full-stack developer.
